I Need Help Now

If you are in immediate danger, call 911. The resources below can help you find support, shelter, and safety.

Crisis Hotlines

National Domestic Violence Hotline 1-800-799-7233 24/7 — Call or text. Confidential support in 200+ languages.
Crisis Text Line Text HOME to 741741 24/7 — Free, confidential crisis support via text message.
California Partnership to End DV 1-800-669-5897 Connects you to local California domestic violence programs and shelters.
988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline 988 24/7 — Call or text for mental health crisis support.

Building a Personal Safety Plan

A personal safety plan is a practical guide you prepare in advance to help protect yourself and your children. Work through each step below at your own pace.

Make a mental note or write down a short list of people you trust, such as a friend, neighbor, coworker, or family member, who you could contact or go to in an emergency. Also identify at least two locations you could go to if you needed to leave quickly, such as a shelter, a trusted person's home, or a public place like a library or police station.

Walk through your home and identify which exits you can use quickly and safely. Think about which rooms have locks, which windows can be opened, and where your car keys are kept. If you have children, decide in advance who gets them and how you would leave together.

In an emergency you may not have access to your phone or contacts list. Memorize or write down on paper the numbers of at least two trusted people, the National Domestic Violence Hotline (1-800-799-7233), and your local shelter or legal aid office. Keep a written copy somewhere accessible but private.

An abusive partner may monitor your phone, email, or location. Consider using a private browser window when researching resources, clearing your browsing history afterward, or using a device they do not have access to. If you use a shared phone plan, be aware that call and text logs may be visible to the account holder.

California law provides several tools to help protect you, including emergency protective orders, domestic violence restraining orders, and custody orders. You do not need an attorney to file for many of these. Visit the Legal Tools section of this site to access the forms you need.

Use the checklist above to pack a bag with your essential documents, medications, cash, and a few days of clothing. Keep it somewhere you can access quickly, such as with a trusted person, in your car, or in a location only you know about. Check it periodically to make sure nothing has expired or needs to be updated.
